Hi there! I'm António Câmara. I'm a junior studying Computer Science and Political Science at Columbia University in New York.

I'm interested in the intersection of artificial intelligence, mathematics, and political science. My research interests include game theory, social choice theory, elections, and public opinion. I am proficient in Python, C, C++, Java, R, and Portuguese.

This summer, I'm working on a project to identify and quantify polarization in categorized corpora of texts, including news sources and social media posts, at the University of Southern California's Information Sciences Institute under Dr. Andrés Abeliuk. This semester, I'm assisting in a study on the formal relationship between democratic backsliding, public opinion, and polarization in the United States and Brazil under Dr. Carlo Prato.

Outside of class, I help run and teach for Harlem Labs, an afterschool introductory computer science program for New York City high school students, I'm a committee member for ADI, Columbia's largest computer science student group, and I serve as a tour guide for Columbia's Engineering Recruitment Committee.
